Section 02 · Global Issue

Heritage vs. Modernity

Bodrum is a place that feels like it's being pulled in two directions. Part of it is old, with ruins, fishing docks, and stone walls that have been standing for thousands of years. The other part is being built over with luxury hotels and expensive marinas. Our question for this trip was: what actually gets lost when one side starts to take over the other?

Heritage Preservation Maritime Culture Sustainable Tourism

2,500+
years of recorded history in Bodrum, but most visitors only go for the beach
  • 🏛️
    Ancient Sites That Are Actually at Risk
    The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus used to be one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, and most of it is gone now. Myndos Gate, where Alexander the Great attacked the city in 334 BC, sits right at the edge of a modern neighborhood. I wanted to understand what it takes to protect places like these when new buildings keep going up around them.
  • ⚓
    What Happens to the Fishing Villages
    Yalıkavak used to be a small fishing town. Now it has one of the most expensive marinas in Turkey. The boats are still there, but the fishermen who used to sell their catch right on the dock have mostly moved away. Walking through both parts of that town in the same afternoon showed me a lot about who actually benefits when a place gets developed.
  • 🌊
    A Museum Built Inside a Castle
    The Bodrum Museum of Underwater Archaeology is inside a Crusader castle from the 1400s. They have Bronze Age shipwrecks, old glassware, and things pulled from the seabed nearby. It was one of those places that made me realize how much history is sitting just below the surface, and how easy it would be to miss it if no one was paying attention.
  • 🧭
    Going as Observers, Not Just Tourists
    We didn't want to just show up, take pictures, and leave. We went with actual questions we wanted to think through. That meant slowing down at places that weren't on the main tourist routes, talking to people when we could, and writing things down while they were still fresh in our heads.
Section 05 · Itinerary

Day-by-Day Plan

This itinerary provides a general overview of our week in Bodrum. The goal was to balance historical exploration, cultural learning, group discussion, and time to experience the local environment.

Day 1 · Arrival & Orientation

Arrival in Bodrum, transfer to Gümüşlük Ümit Villa, group orientation, introduction to the trip theme, and an evening walk through the local area.

Day 2 · Ancient Bodrum

Visit major historical landmarks including Bodrum Castle, the Museum of Underwater Archaeology, and the Bodrum waterfront while discussing the city's long history.

Day 3 · Wonders of the Ancient World

Explore the Mausoleum at Halicarnassus, Ancient Theatre, and Myndos Gate. Focus on heritage preservation and the challenges of protecting ancient sites.

Day 4 · Coastal Communities

Visit Gümüşlük and Yalıkavak to compare traditional fishing culture with modern tourism development and luxury marina expansion.

Day 5 · Orak Island Excursion

Full-day boat excursion to Orak Island. Observe natural coastal environments and discuss sustainable tourism and environmental stewardship.

Day 6 · Reflection & Exploration

Additional site visits, independent observation activities, photography, journaling, and group discussions connecting experiences to the project's global issue.

Day 7 · Synthesis & Departure Preparation

Final reflections, review of key findings, discussion of SDGs, cultural lessons learned, and preparation for departure.

Section 04 · Dining Profiles

Where We Ate

We tried to pick restaurants that actually said something about the area, not just places that showed up first in a search. Some of them have been around for decades. Some were hidden enough that you'd never find them by accident. All of them were worth going to.

Breakfasts

Limon Gümüşlük
Up on a hillside above the bay, with a garden that gets the morning sun. We had breakfast here looking out at the water through olive trees. It was probably the most relaxed start to any day on the trip, and nobody was in a rush to leave.
Breakfast
Teldolap Café & Restaurant
A cliffside terrace with a view over the water. We ordered something simple and then just sat there for way longer than planned because no one wanted to move. That kind of place.
Breakfast

Lunches

Avlu Bodrum
Hidden inside an old stone building in the center of town. The courtyard is shaded and cool even when it's hot outside. You could walk past the entrance ten times without noticing it was there.
Lunch
Antik Akdeniz
Wood-fired pides, the real kind. This is the sort of place where locals actually eat, not just tourists. Nothing fancy, just good food at fair prices in the old part of town.
Lunch
Kocadon Restaurant
A 19th-century villa that became a restaurant at some point, and you can still tell. Stone archways, vines overhead, the whole thing. The mezze spread here was honestly one of the best meals of the whole week.
Lunch
Sünger Pizza
Been open since 1992, right on the harbour. Half the town eats here. The pizza is thin and crispy and not what you'd expect at all, in a good way. It's been there so long it feels like part of the city.
Lunch
Kitchenette Yalıkavak
A café stop at the marina. Good for a midday break when the group needed to sit down, eat something decent, and figure out the plan for the afternoon.
Lunch
La Pasion Cafe
A small courtyard tucked into one of Bodrum's quieter side streets. Colorful walls, mismatched furniture, really good food. It felt more like eating at someone's home than at a restaurant.
Lunch

Dinners

Frenk Bodrum
Right on the harbour, busy and loud in a good way. We went here on the first night, which worked well. It helped everyone ease into Bodrum before we started getting into the heavier historical sites the next morning.
Dinner
Aquarium Balık Restaurant
You eat fish here with sunken ancient ruins visible in the water right next to your table. That's not an exaggeration, they're just there a few metres away. One of the stranger and more memorable meals of the whole trip.
Dinner
Yarbasan Restaurant
Built to look like a traditional Turkish stone village, with individual buildings and winding paths between them. It sounds like it might be overdone, but it actually works. The food is serious and the atmosphere is unlike any other restaurant we went to.
Dinner
Windmills Dağbelen
Up on a mountain ridge above the city. You can see the whole peninsula from up there, the bays, the islands, the marina lights. We went for sunset and honestly nobody wanted to come back down after.
Dinner
Sait Restaurant
Waterfront at Yalıkavak Marina. Calm, unhurried, a bit more upscale. It was a good night to sit down together as a group after a full day and actually talk through what we'd been seeing all week.
Dinner
Berk Balık Restaurant
Right on the beach, facing the castle. At night the whole fortress is lit up and it reflects across the water. We saved this one for the last evening on purpose. It felt like the right way to end the trip.
Dinner

Section 04 · UN Sustainable Development Goals

How This Trip Supports the SDGs

This cultural and historical exploration connects directly to several of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). By studying heritage preservation, sustainable tourism, and local communities, students gain a deeper understanding of how global issues affect real places.

🏫 SDG 4 — Quality Education

Students learn through direct observation of historical sites, museums, and local communities, turning travel into an educational experience beyond the classroom.

🏙️ SDG 11 — Sustainable Cities & Communities

The trip explores how Bodrum balances urban development, tourism growth, and the protection of important cultural heritage sites.

🌍 SDG 12 — Responsible Consumption & Production

By examining tourism's impact on local communities and natural environments, students consider how visitors can travel more responsibly.

🌊 SDG 14 — Life Below Water

Visits to the Museum of Underwater Archaeology and coastal areas highlight the importance of protecting marine ecosystems and underwater cultural heritage.

🤝 SDG 17 — Partnerships for the Goals

Learning from local communities, historians, museum staff, and tourism professionals demonstrates how collaboration helps preserve cultural heritage for future generations.
